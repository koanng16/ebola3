    Effects of Ebola

    The Ebola outbreak which began in Guinea earlier this year continues to spread uncontrollably throughout West Africa,the World Health Organization reports nearly 9,000 cases with 4,500 deaths.Ebola is not well known or understood by communities in the affected countries.The virus can even attack the connective tissues that are rapidly multiplying in collagen. Collagen is responsible for keeping the organs in place, and the virus destroys and digests such tissues.It also causes blood clots in the bloodstream, so the blood thickens and the blood flow weakens. These clots tend to get stuck in the blood vessels, which in turn causes the red spots on the skin. As the disease progresses, the spots get bigger. The blood clots will also slow down the blood supply to most organs of the body such as the lungs, brain, liver, intestines, kidneys, testicles, and breast tissues. All these organs will become severely damaged and so, will eventually stop functioning. Because of the many devastating Ebola virus effects on the body, death may be caused by shock, renal failure, or loss of blood.


    Ebola can be spread by having contact with body fluids, like blood, sweat, etc. It can also be spread by objects like needles that has been contaminated by infected patients.

    Progress of ebola is it is left untreated
    • From days 5-9
      There would be fatigue, headache, fever and chills
    • Around day 10
      There would be high fever, vomiting, blood, rash, passive behavior.
    • Around day 11
      In some cases there would be some bleeding from mouth, eyes, nose and anus
    • Around day 12
      There may be internal bleeding, loss of conciousness, and death.
