    What is Ebola?

    Ebola virus disease (EVD), formerly known as Ebola haemorrhagic fever, is an infectious and generally fatal disease marked by fever and severe internal bleeding, spread through contact with infected body fluids by a filovirus ( Ebola virus ), whose normal host species is unknown. The virus is transmitted to people from wild animals and spreads in the human population through human-to-human transmission.
